Flowers from the Storm Laura Kinsale October 1992 The Duke of Jervaulx was a dangerous and intelligent man. He was referred to as the "D of J" in gossip sheets, which frequently featured him and his numerous antics. He was seen as dissolute, careless, and wasteful. The sheltered Maddy Timms, the calm daughter of a straightforward mathematician, was intrigued by even his most casual attentions, proving that even the most womanizing rake may occasionally be alluring.
The Tragedy of Romeo and Juliet William Shakespeare 1597 A long-standing feud between two wealthy families turns violent. A gang of masked Montagues break into a Capulet celebration, risking additional strife. A young Romeo Montague who is infatuated with love falls in love right away with Juliet Capulet, who is about to wed the man of her father's choosing, the County Paris.
The Hunchback of Notre-Dame Victor Hugo 1883 It tells the tale of Quasimodo, a bell ringer for the Notre Dame Cathedral who is disfigured, and his love for the lovely gypsy Esmeralda. Quasimodo tries to help Esmeralda when she is labeled a witch by Claude Frollo, the tortured archdeacon who fancies her. But, his motives are misinterpreted.
Gone with the Wind Margaret Mitchell June 30, 1936 The Civil War, starvation, rape, murder, heartbreak, and enslavement are all themes in the novel Gone With the Wind. One might not automatically associate this book with hope. Scarlett O'Hara, one of the most ruthlessly optimistic characters in literature, is at the center of the book.
The Rebecca Notebook: and Other Memories Daphne du Maurier 1980 The plot concerns a young woman who marries an intriguing widower only to learn that she must live in the shadow of his late wife Rebecca, who passed away unexplainably a number of years before. The young wife must come to terms with her attractive, cold husband Max De Winter's dreadful secret (Laurence Olivier). She also has to cope with the housekeeper Mrs. Danvers (Judith Anderson), who is envious and obnoxious and won't recognize her as the mistress of the house.
